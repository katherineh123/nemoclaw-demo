Transfer Scenario: Move 50 Workers from Asia → Germany

Impact analysis on both factories | 6-month projection from May 2026

Executive Summary

Transferring 50 workers from Asia to Germany would bring the Germany factory above 70% by June 2026 (70.9%) and up to 82.2% by August — well into the "At Risk" and then near "On Track" zone.


However, this comes at a severe cost to the Asia factory, which would collapse to ~45% fulfillment — deep in the Critical zone and far below its current ~63%.


⚠️ Trade-off Warning: The fixed headcount pool of 450 means Germany's gain is Asia's loss. Moving 50 workers fixes Germany but puts Asia into crisis. You may want to consider a smaller transfer or explore the longer-lead net-new hiring process.

Key Takeaways

  • Germany reaches 70%+ by June 2026 (month 2 after transfer) as the 50 transferred workers begin ramping up.
  • Germany plateaus at ~82% once all transferred workers become tenured (month 3+).
  • Asia drops to ~45-47% — a catastrophic decline from its current ~61%. This would require immediate escalation.
  • Net effect: You fix one factory but break the other. The combined fulfillment across both sites would be worse.
  • Alternative to consider: A smaller transfer (e.g. 20-30 workers) might get Germany above 70% without devastating Asia, or net-new hiring could avoid the zero-sum constraint entirely.
