📋 Analysis Summary

At current headcount (257 workers), the Germany factory is projected to fulfill approximately 64.7% of orders on time in May 2026, placing it in the 🔴 Critical zone. Even as recent hires gain experience over the next few months, fulfillment plateaus at ~67.3% — still below your 70% minimum threshold.

The model shows that tenured workers (3+ months experience) are by far the strongest driver of on-time delivery (coefficient: 0.299 per worker), while brand-new hires contribute almost nothing immediately (0.018). This means simply hiring new workers won't help next month — you need experienced hands.

⚠️ Recommendation: You need to add at least 18 experienced/tenured workers to the Germany factory to reach the 70% fulfillment threshold next month. Given the fixed 450-person pool across both sites, this would mean transferring ~18 tenured workers from the Asia factory (reducing it from ~200 to ~182). With this transfer, Germany would project at 70.1% in May and climb to ~72.6% by July as remaining newer workers gain tenure. However, note this would likely impact Asia factory performance — a trade-off worth evaluating.
